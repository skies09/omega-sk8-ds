Complete Decks

When buying a skateboard, you can either buy a complete skateboard where all the parts have already been assembled for you, or you can build a custom skateboard choosing all the individual components based on your preferences.

Please note that custom building your own skateboard is not recommended by novices/beginners

Below is a helpful guide to the skateboard parts required for building your own custom skateboard.
If you are still unsure, please contact us and we would be happy to help and make recommendations.

Skateboard Parts

Deck

The deck is the board you ride on.

Grip Tape

This is a sandpaper-like material that is stuck to the top of your deck. It is completely anti-slip which allows you to grip the top of the board as you ride.

Wheels

The skateboard has 4 wheels. They are rubber wheels that come in a variety of sizes.
Large Wheels VS Small Wheels:
The wheel diameter is measured in mm. Smaller wheels are slower, while larger wheels are faster.
Larger wheels have a greater top speed but take longer and more effort to accelerate.
Smaller wheels accelerate quicker but have a lower top speed.
The most standard setup is 50mm-55mm wheel.

Trucks

A pair of trucks is required to link the deck and the wheels.
The trucks also assist with turning the board. More flex in the trucks means the board is easier to turn, where as less flex gives more stability.
The width of your trucks should correspond with the width of your deck, with the tip of each axle level with the deck edge. (See image below)

Bearings

Skateboard bearings are used to mount the wheel to the axle.
